What is the student-guided punting tour?

It is a shared chauffeured punt along the College Backs, poled and narrated by a current Cambridge University student rather than a full-time professional chauffeur. That is what makes it the cheapest seat on the river, and the reason it feels different from the other tours.

You sit in a flat wooden punt with a handful of other travellers, push off from one of the licensed stations, and drift behind the old colleges while your guide does the work with a long pole. There is no paddling and no balance to worry about. The route is the same classic stretch of the Cam the other chauffeured tours run, so you are not trading away the view to save money.

What you are gaining is the voice. A student who actually studies here points out which college they walk past to lectures, what the porters are like, where exams happen, the small stuff a brochure never tells you. The history is all there too, just delivered by someone living it rather than reciting it.

What you'll see on the College Backs

The tour runs the College Backs, the stretch of the River Cam behind the old colleges, and it is the view Cambridge is famous for. From the water you look up at the colleges and slip under bridges you simply cannot reach on foot. Read the full route in our College Backs guide.

  1. Meet at the station
    Cambridge licenses six punting stations. Your booking shows the meeting point; arrive a few minutes early.
  2. Step aboard and push off
    Take a seat in the shared punt. Your student guide handles the pole, so there is nothing to learn.
  3. King's College and Clare
    The view of King's College Chapel from the water, then Clare College and its quiet stone bridge.
  4. Trinity and St John's
    Past Trinity, then St John's and the covered Bridge of Sighs, with the stories told as you glide.
  5. The Mathematical Bridge
    The famous wooden bridge at Queens', and the legend that comes with it, before you head back.

The Backs run behind the historic colleges on the River Cam. More background at Wikipedia: The Backs and on the Mathematical Bridge.

Student-guided punting FAQ

Is the student-guided punt really the cheapest tour?

It is the lowest-priced shared seat on the Cam and the best value of the three tours we feature, because a Cambridge University student poles the punt rather than a full-time professional chauffeur. Prices are dynamic, so check the live price on the listing first, and compare it on our comparison page.

What makes the student-guided tour different?

Your guide is a current Cambridge University student who poles the punt and tells the stories from the inside. You get a student's-eye view of the colleges alongside the usual history, at the lowest shared price, on the same College Backs route as the other chauffeured tours.

How long is the tour and where does it go?

About 45 to 50 minutes along the College Backs. You glide past King's, Clare, Trinity and St John's, and under the Bridge of Sighs and the Mathematical Bridge. The full route is in our College Backs guide.

Is a 4.9 rating from only 38 reviews reliable?

The 4.9 out of 5 is real and taken from the operator's GetYourGuide listing, but 38 reviews is a smaller, newer sample than our other tours, which have hundreds or thousands. Read the strong score as a promising early signal rather than a long, settled track record.

Do I share the punt with other people?

Yes. This is a shared chauffeured tour, so you sit with other travellers and split the cost, which is what makes it the cheapest seat. If you want the punt to yourselves, choose the private tour instead.
