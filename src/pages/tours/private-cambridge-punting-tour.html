What is the private Cambridge punting tour?

It is a chauffeured punt reserved for your group alone, running about 45 to 50 minutes along the College Backs. A professional chauffeur handles the pole and the commentary, and no strangers share your boat.

The Backs are the stretch of the River Cam behind the old colleges, and it is the view Cambridge is famous for. On a private tour the difference is not the route, it is the room. You set the pace, you decide when to slow down for a photo, and the guide talks to your group rather than a boatful of strangers.

That makes it the relaxed, unhurried version of the classic Cambridge experience. The chauffeur does all the work, you sit on the cushioned seats and look up at King's College Chapel from the water. What you'll see along the College Backs

The tour runs the College Backs, the river stretch behind the old colleges, taking in King's, Clare, Trinity and St John's, and gliding under the Bridge of Sighs and past the Mathematical Bridge. The chauffeur times the route so the famous views land well and shares the history as you go.

  1. King's College and the chapel
    The headline view, looking up at King's College Chapel from the water.
  2. Clare College and Clare Bridge
    The oldest of the college bridges on the Backs, with its stone balls and quiet symmetry.
  3. Trinity College
    The largest college, with stories the guide will know by heart.
  4. St John's and the Bridge of Sighs
    Gliding past the covered Bridge of Sighs, one of the most photographed spots on the river.
  5. The Mathematical Bridge
    The famous wooden Mathematical Bridge at Queens', and the legend that goes with it.

Private punt: the honest pros and cons

A private tour buys you privacy, flexibility and a guide who is focused on your group. The trade-off is cost: you book the whole punt, so it is dearer than a shared seat.

Why people pick private

  • The boat is yours: no strangers, no sharing the chauffeur's attention
  • Flexible timing and pace, with room to stop for photos
  • The easiest option for families with children
  • A popular choice for proposals, anniversaries and birthdays
  • Top-rated: 4.9 out of 5 across 339 Viator reviews

Worth knowing first

  • Priced per punt, so it costs more than a shared seat
  • Best value when your group fills the boat, not for solo travellers
  • Open-air: bring layers and check the forecast
  • Summer weekends are busiest; book ahead for the slot you want

On a tight budget or travelling solo? A shared chauffeured tour gives you the same route at a lower per-person price.

What's it really like? Jordan's take

I have poled the Backs since 2021, and the private tours are the ones I remember. On a shared punt you are working the whole boat; on a private one you can read your group and slow down where it matters. Families relax because the kids are not perched next to strangers. Couples get the quiet stretch under the Bridge of Sighs to themselves.

My honest advice: tell the chauffeur if it is a birthday, an anniversary or a proposal, because we will time the route and the photos around it. Go early morning or late afternoon if you can, when the river is calmest and the light on King's Chapel is at its best. And book ahead in summer, because the good private slots go first.

Private Cambridge punting FAQ

What is a private Cambridge punting tour?

It is a chauffeured punt booked for your group only. A professional chauffeur poles the punt and tells the stories while you sit back, and no strangers share the boat. How much does a private punting tour cost?

A private tour is priced per punt rather than per person, so it costs more than a shared seat but can work out reasonable for a small group. Is a private punting tour good for families?

Yes. A private punt is often the easiest option for a family. The chauffeur handles all the punting, the seated ride suits children and older travellers, and having the boat to yourselves means you set the pace and can stop for photos without holding up strangers.

Can you propose or celebrate on a private punt?

A private punt is a popular choice for proposals, anniversaries and birthdays because you have the boat and the guide's attention to yourselves. Mention the occasion when you book so the chauffeur can help with timing and photos, and confirm any extras on the official listing.

What will you see on the tour?

The route runs along the College Backs on the River Cam. You pass King's College and its chapel, Clare College and Clare Bridge, Trinity College and St John's College, and glide under the Bridge of Sighs and past the Mathematical Bridge. See the full route in our College Backs guide.

When is the best time to book a private punt?

Punting runs through the season, busiest in summer and calmer in spring and autumn. A private tour gives you a guaranteed slot for your group, which matters most on summer weekends. Early morning and late afternoon are the quietest, prettiest times on the water.
