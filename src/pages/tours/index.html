What every tour has in common

All three are guided, last about 45 to 50 minutes, run the College Backs on the River Cam, and leave from one of Cambridge's six licensed punting stations. Whichever you pick, someone else handles the pole while you sit back, so there is no balance to learn and no chance of getting wet.

The Backs is the stretch of river behind the old colleges, and it is the view Cambridge is famous for. On every tour you glide past King's College, the Bridge of Sighs and the Mathematical Bridge, with the history told as you go. The differences are who you share the punt with, who poles it, and the price.

Cambridge punting tours FAQ

Which Cambridge punting tour is best?

For most visitors the shared chauffeured tour is the best mix of price and experience: a professional chauffeur poles the punt and tells the stories while you relax past the College Backs. Pick the private tour for a date, family or occasion, and the student-guided shared tour for the cheapest seat. See the full comparison.

What do all three punting tours have in common?

Every tour here runs the classic College Backs route on the River Cam, lasts about 45 to 50 minutes, and leaves from one of the six licensed Cambridge punting stations. On each one you pass King's College, the Bridge of Sighs and the Mathematical Bridge, and you sit while someone else does the punting.

Shared, private or student-guided: which should I choose?

Choose the shared chauffeured tour for the best value and a professional guide, the private tour when you want the punt to yourselves, and the student-guided shared tour for the cheapest seat and a current Cambridge student's take on the city. The shared tour is the most booked; the private and student tours both rate highest at 4.9.

How much do Cambridge punting tours cost?

Prices change by season, date and group size, and the three tours are priced differently, so we do not quote a fixed figure. The shared and student-guided tours are the most affordable seats, while a private tour is priced per punt and costs more. Always check the live price on the official listing. Full breakdown in our prices guide.

Do you need to book a punting tour in advance?

Off-peak you can often just turn up at a punting station and join the next shared tour. In summer and on weekends the popular slots fill up, so booking ahead through the official listing guarantees your time and shows the live price first.
